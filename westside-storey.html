Antique Shop for Generation Y

Chris Harrington

History is precious and can teach us a lot about our past. Sometimes, it can even give us a glimpse into the future. With this in mind Chris Harrington, the founder of Westside Storey, took a risk opening a small antique shop in an underdeveloped area with lots of potential. This bustling area known as 17th and Summit, is located in the Westside neighborhood and is creating a stir around the arts district of downtown Kansas City.

Imagine the typical antique store experience. What do you see? A cluttered, claustrophobic catastrophe with quantity over quality. Westside Storey is trying to change the face of antique shopping, aiming to please a younger, hipper audience that frequents the 17th street area. Chris has a keen eye for incorporating items that interest visitors of all age groups without oversaturating his retail space.

As antiques come and go, you can expect a new visual experience with each visit to Westside Storey. Chris takes the opportunity to rearrange the display in a new way every time an item is purchased. Using a thematic approach to merchandising, he creates visual landscapes that spark the imagination.

Chris Harrington’s genuine interest in antiques is contagious to his customers and you will be hard-pressed to leave the store empty handed. His passion for objects with a history stems from spending time at his grandmother’s house exploring her possessions. This passion is communicated through his charismatic personality and translates into a unique shopping experience.

Chris is joined by co-owners Ellen Trakas and Kay Brittingham. The store opened its doors in April of this year.
